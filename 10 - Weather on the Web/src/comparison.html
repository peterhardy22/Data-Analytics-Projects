Latitude vs. Weather Condition

Click any plot to get an in-depth analysis.

Max Temperature

As expected the temperature seems to be at its highest the closer to the equator that a city is located. As a city starts to move farther away from the equator, cities on average tend to become less warm. The fact that the southern hemisphere (lat under 0) are on average slightly warmer than northern hemisphere (lat over 0) cities based on the tilt of the earth. during this time of year the southern part of the earth is closer to the sun, hence warmer temperatures.

Humidity

This plot helps show that humidity is somewhat undertermined by a cities latitude. Across the earth humidity seems to vary for reason other than latitude. Based on the graph it does appear northern hemisphere cities are slightly more likely to be humid, however the data is somewhat spread. What you could draw as a conclusion is that most cities around the world generaly live in at least 60% humidity.

Cloudiness

Like the graph on humidity, this graph for how cloudiness is effected by a cities latitude is completely evenly spread. Clouds show to have no effect based on a cities latitude. What causes cloudniess is definitely connected to other variables besides a cities latitude.

Wind Speed

Wind speed seems to show the opposite of the humidity chart in that most cities seem to have relatively low wind speeds. Again, we cannot draw the conclusion that a cities latitude effects its windiness. Other factors are definitely at play for creating windiness in a city.
